The Tiger and the Sloth

Part 1

There once was a Sloth, who was a little bit slow,
And a fearsome Tiger, would that Sloth come to know,
From across the forest, they gamed and swapped words,
Until the Tiger at last agreed to converge.
The Tiger and Sloth had a jolly good time,
The hills, the lakes, and even the country's outline!
And ofcourse when they traveled, the Tiger had it her way,
“If you were in charge of direction, we'd be dead in a day!”
But then the Sloth hurt his skin, and things started to sour,
And their differences meant they were easy to anger,
And the Sloth was lazy, enraging the Tiger more,
“Put in more effort, or I'll head for the door!”
But the Tiger stuck by him, for reasons unknown,
Within one another they had both found a home,
They completed the other, made up for each's flaws,
And help each other grow until they both had in-laws.
They were a team after all, and neither would sway,
The Tiger and Sloth, on hay they would lay,
“Play with my hair!” the Tiger would say,
“Play with my hair like you did on that day!”
Their love for each other would not wither away,
And whoever messed with the duo would pay,
Their souls were bound tighter than a wedding bouquet,
And their bond would last longer than radioactive decay.
